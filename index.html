Valencia: Parks, Markets + City Heart

Sunday · Mercado Central · Turia Park · Gulliver · El Carmen
Start early at Mercado Central, then Horchatería Santa Catalina, the Cathedral exterior, and Turia Park with Gulliver in the afternoon. Valencia is flat, walkable, and built for families.
🧒 With Kids
  • Mercado Central: arrive by 8:30am before crowds. Stroller works. Incredible sensory experience.
  • Turia Park is perfect for strollers — flat paved path on a dried riverbed. Rent a cargo bike or 4-seater pedal car near Gulliver.
  • Gulliver Park is free — giant Gulliver lets kids climb and slide all over him. Arrive before 10am.
  • Horchatería Santa Catalina: kids love horchata — sweet, cold, completely unlike anything at home
🍷 Without Kids
  • Cathedral Micalet tower: 207 narrow steps — skip with baby carrier
  • Mercado Central deep browse alone: sample and buy without managing a stroller
  • Ruzafa evening solo — the neighborhood has a local energy that's hard to feel with kids

Playa El Saler + La Albufera

Tuesday · Best Valencia Beach · Rice Paddies · El Palmar paella
Go south. Playa El Saler is the better beach — quieter, cleaner, pine shade nearby. La Albufera's rice paddy landscape is one of the most surreal things in Spain. El Palmar village is where Valencia paella actually comes from.
🧒 With Kids
  • El Saler: Blue Flag, calmer water, pine tree shade access, easier parking than Malvarrosa
  • Rent umbrella + 2 loungers from the chiringuito (~€15) — worth it for the shade
  • La Albufera: bring mosquito repellent. Wetland environment. Non-negotiable with babies.
  • El Palmar village: outdoor terraces, kids can roam, relaxed pace
🍷 Without Kids
  • Albufera boat tour: small wooden boat across the lagoon (€5-8, 30 min) — peaceful and atmospheric
  • Malvarrosa promenade has more adult city beach energy as an alternative

Old Town + La Concha Beach

Friday · First full Basque day · Pintxos crawl · La Concha swim
La Concha is one of the great urban beaches in the world — a perfect protected bay, almost no waves, lifeguard all summer. The Parte Vieja pintxos scene is the best eating experience in Europe.
🧒 With Kids
  • La Concha: protected bay with almost zero waves. The shallowest, safest beach in Spain for young kids. Lifeguard all summer.
  • Paseo de la Concha promenade is flat and stroller-perfect — 8-min walk from Arbaso
  • San Telmo Museum: stroller-accessible, kids often respond to the soaring main hall
  • Parte Vieja morning (before 10am): stroller manageable when streets are quiet
🍷 Without Kids — Evening Pintxos Crawl
  • Standing at bars with a stroller is genuinely difficult. Send one parent with the kids while the other does the crawl at 7pm.
  • Route: Bar Txepetxa → Ganbara → La Cuchara → A Fuego Negro → La Viña
  • Budget €20-25 and 90 minutes. One pintxo per bar, move when you've had it.
